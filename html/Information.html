2. Eco-Drive

-The watch uses Eco-Drive technology, does not use batteries, runs entirely on energy generated from any light source, from sunlight or natural, artificial light. Using the mechanisms of a solar panel and a battery-charging device, light is converted into energy and stored in order to keep the watch going. The Eco-Drive meter is fully automatic in recharging, the user only needs to expose the watch to light sources, preferably sunlight, the watch will absorb and switch sources. light into energy. When fully charged, the watch can run in the dark from 6 months to 1 year, depending on the model. No batteries, no winding, no waving hands, it's a watch that doesn't bother you.


3. Automatic Watch

A non-PIN winding-powered watch movement, the automatic energy is generated by an energy source from the main winding connected to a weight wheel (yes Crescent). The movement of the wearer's wrist causes the dumbbell to spin and wind the watch. Energy is transferred to the needle shaft on the dial and the time is indicated by the hands. There are two popular types of automatic watches today:


a). Handwinding "Manual winding": The type of watch in which the wearer must use the hand to turn the knob to wind the winding at a certain time is usually specified how many hours or days (Number of turns to wind (depending on watch type).
b). Automatic "Automatic winding": The watch is wound by hand movement of the wearer. Based on the principle of earth's gravity, a rotor rotates and transmits its energy to the spring through a suitable mechanism. This means that the watch does not need to be manually recharged and, unlike a quartz watch, does not require the use of batteries. This system was invented by the Swiss inventor Abraham-Louis Perrelet in the 18th century. The movements usually consist of many jewels such as 17, 21, 25.
